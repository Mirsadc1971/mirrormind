# MirrorMind 365-Day Archetype Growth Plans
### A Full-Year Behavioural Transformation Protocol for Each Archetype

**Version 1.0 — March 2026**

---

## How the Year Is Structured

Every 365-day plan follows the same four-quarter architecture, but the *content* of each quarter is entirely specific to the archetype's shadow pattern and growth direction.

| Quarter | Theme | Weeks | Core Work |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Q1 — Awareness** | Seeing clearly | 1–13 | Identify the pattern, name the shadow, establish baseline habits |
| **Q2 — Disruption** | Breaking the pattern | 14–26 | Actively interrupt the default behaviour, build new responses |
| **Q3 — Integration** | Making it structural | 27–39 | Embed new behaviours into identity and relationships |
| **Q4 — Expansion** | Living differently | 40–52 | Extend growth into the hardest areas; consolidate the year |

Each quarter contains:
- **Monthly focus themes** (3 per quarter)
- **Weekly habits** (specific, behavioural, measurable)
- **Monthly milestone** (a concrete marker of progress)
- **Quarter-end integration checkpoint** (a reflective review with the AI Twin)

The **Weekly Check-In** (3 questions via the AI Twin) runs throughout the entire year:
1. What did I do differently this week?
2. What did I avoid?
3. What did I notice about myself?

## Archetype 1 — The Architect
*"You build systems to avoid feeling. This year, you learn to act before certainty arrives."*

**Growth Direction:** From deliberate paralysis → toward decisive trust.

---

### Q1 — Awareness (Weeks 1–13): Seeing the Blueprint

The Architect's first task is not to change — it is to see the pattern with precision. They are already excellent at analysis; the work is to turn that analytical lens on their own avoidance.

**Month 1 — Mapping the Delay**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Each evening, list one decision you did not make today. Write the reason you gave yourself. | Surface the rationalisation pattern |
| 2 | For each delayed decision, write: "If I had all the information I think I need, would I actually decide?" | Expose the false premise |
| 3 | Identify the oldest unresolved decision in your life. Write it down. Do not resolve it yet — just name it. | Confront the backlog |
| 4 | Ask one person you trust: "What do you think I'm avoiding?" Listen without defending. | External calibration |

**Monthly Milestone:** You can name your three most-avoided decisions and the specific fear underneath each one.

**Month 2 — Understanding the Cost**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Write a one-page account of one opportunity you lost because you waited too long. Be specific. | Make the cost concrete |
| 6 | Track every time you say "I need to think about it" or "I need more information." Count the instances. | Quantify the pattern |
| 7 | For each instance tracked, ask: "What was I actually afraid of?" | Connect behaviour to emotion |
| 8 | Write the answer to: "What would the version of me who trusts themselves do right now?" | Introduce the growth identity |

**Monthly Milestone:** You have a clear, written account of what analysis paralysis has cost you in one specific domain.

**Month 3 — Baseline Establishment**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 9 | Make one low-stakes decision per day in under 60 seconds. Log the outcome. | Build decision muscle |
| 10 | Identify your "information threshold" — the point at which you have enough to decide. Write it down for three current decisions. | Create a decision rule |
| 11 | Share one opinion in a group setting without qualifying it. Note the response. | Practice unhedged expression |
| 12 | Write your Q1 reflection: What pattern did you see? What surprised you? What are you still avoiding? | Consolidate awareness |
| 13 | **Q1 Integration Checkpoint with AI Twin:** Review the three oldest avoided decisions. Choose one to resolve in Q2. | Transition to action |

---

### Q2 — Disruption (Weeks 14–26): Breaking the Blueprint

Q2 is the hardest quarter for the Architect. It requires acting before certainty — the thing their entire personality has been built to avoid.

**Month 4 — The 60-Second Decision**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 14 | Make one medium-stakes decision per week in under 60 seconds. No research permitted. | Interrupt the research reflex |
| 15 | Resolve the avoided decision chosen in the Q1 checkpoint. Act on it this week. | Break the oldest pattern |
| 16 | When you feel the urge to research further, set a 10-minute timer. Decide when it ends. | Create a time constraint |
| 17 | Tell one person about a decision you made quickly. Notice if you feel the need to justify it. | Observe the approval pattern |

**Monthly Milestone:** You have resolved one decision that had been pending for more than three months.

**Month 5 — Emotional Vocabulary**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 18 | Each evening, name one emotion you experienced today. Not a thought — a feeling. | Build emotional literacy |
| 19 | When you feel uncomfortable, write: "The feeling is \_\_\_. The thought I'm using to avoid it is \_\_\_." | Separate feeling from rationalisation |
| 20 | Have one conversation this week where you lead with a feeling rather than an analysis. | Practise emotional expression |
| 21 | Ask someone close to you: "Do you feel like you know what I'm feeling, or just what I'm thinking?" | External emotional calibration |

**Monthly Milestone:** You can consistently name the emotion underneath a decision delay without immediately converting it to analysis.

**Month 6 — Imperfect Action**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 22 | Do one thing this week that you know is "good enough" rather than optimal. Submit it. | Practise sufficiency |
| 23 | Start a project with an intentionally imperfect plan. Document the discomfort. | Disrupt the perfectionism reflex |
| 24 | When you catch yourself revising something for the third time, stop and submit it. | Create a revision limit |
| 25 | Write: "The cost of imperfection in this situation is \_\_\_. The cost of delay is \_\_\_." Compare them. | Reframe the risk calculus |
| 26 | **Q2 Integration Checkpoint:** What decisions did you make quickly? What happened? What are you still over-preparing for? | Consolidate disruption |

---

### Q3 — Integration (Weeks 27–39): Building a New Architecture

Q3 is about making the new behaviour structural — embedding it into systems, relationships, and identity so it does not require constant willpower.

**Month 7 — Decision Systems**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 27 | Create a personal decision framework: three criteria that are "enough" for a yes or no. | Replace research with rules |
| 28 | Apply the framework to every decision this week. Note where it fails and why. | Test and refine |
| 29 | Share the framework with someone who knows you well. Ask if it sounds like you or like who you want to be. | Accountability and calibration |
| 30 | Identify one area of life where you still have no framework. Build one. | Extend the system |

**Monthly Milestone:** You have a working personal decision framework that you have applied to at least ten decisions.

**Month 8 — Relational Integration**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 31 | Tell one person about your growth work this year. Not the insights — the actions. | Make it relational |
| 32 | Ask a close friend or partner: "Have you noticed a difference in how I make decisions?" | External validation of change |
| 33 | Have one conversation where you express uncertainty without immediately resolving it. | Practise tolerating ambiguity in relationship |
| 34 | Write: "The relationships that my old pattern was protecting me from are \_\_\_." | Connect pattern to relational cost |

**Monthly Milestone:** At least one person in your life has noticed and named a change in how you operate.

**Month 9 — Identity Consolidation**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 35 | Write a new self-description: "I am someone who \_\_\_" — defined by action, not analysis. | Anchor the new identity |
| 36 | Identify the last remaining domain where the old pattern is still dominant. Name it. | Find the final frontier |
| 37 | Make one significant decision in that domain using your framework. | Apply the new identity to the hardest case |
| 38 | Write your Q3 reflection: Who are you becoming? What is the old version of you still doing? | Consolidate integration |
| 39 | **Q3 Integration Checkpoint:** Review the year so far. What has changed in your decisions, relationships, and self-perception? | Prepare for expansion |

---

### Q4 — Expansion (Weeks 40–52): Living the New Architecture

Q4 is about extending the growth into the hardest areas — the relationships, commitments, and risks that the old pattern was specifically designed to avoid.

**Month 10 — The Hardest Commitment**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 40 | Identify the commitment you have been avoiding for the longest time. Name it. | Surface the final avoidance |
| 41 | Make the commitment. Tell someone. | Act before certainty |
| 42 | When the urge to over-prepare arrives, use your decision framework instead. | Apply the year's work to the hardest case |
| 43 | Write: "I used to need \_\_\_ before I could commit. Now I need \_\_\_." | Document the shift |

**Monthly Milestone:** You have made one significant commitment that the January version of you would not have made.

**Month 11 — Spontaneity as Practice**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 44 | Once per week, do something completely unplanned. No research, no preparation. | Build spontaneity as a skill |
| 45 | Say yes to one invitation this week without asking any clarifying questions first. | Practise trust |
| 46 | Have one conversation where you share what you want before you share what you think. | Lead with desire, not analysis |
| 47 | Write: "The things I used to plan that I now just do are \_\_\_." | Inventory the growth |

**Monthly Milestone:** Spontaneous action has become a regular, unremarkable part of your week.

**Month 12 — Year-End Integration**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 48 | Re-read your Week 1 journal entry. Write a response from who you are now. | Measure the distance |
| 49 | Ask three people who know you well: "What is the biggest change you have seen in me this year?" | External year-end audit |
| 50 | Write your year-end reflection: What did you build? What did you stop avoiding? What remains? | Consolidate the year |
| 51 | Design your Year 2 focus: one area where the growth needs to continue. | Create continuity |
| 52 | **Year-End Integration Checkpoint with AI Twin:** Full review. What is the new baseline? What is the next frontier? | Close the year |

## Archetype 2 — The Performer
*"You perform so well that no one — including you — knows what you actually want. This year, you find out."*

**Growth Direction:** From self-erasure for approval → toward self-definition independent of others.

---

| 3 | Identify the three people whose approval matters most to you. Write why. | Name the audience |
| 4 | Ask yourself: "What would I do this week if none of those three people would ever find out?" | Separate desire from performance |

**Monthly Milestone:** You can name five things you want that you have never said out loud.

**Month 2 — Understanding the Cost**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Write a one-page account of a time you gave someone what they wanted instead of what you wanted. What did it cost you? | Make the cost concrete |
| 6 | Identify one relationship where you feel most "performed." Write what the real version of you would say in that relationship. | Name the gap |
| 7 | Notice every time you say "I'm fine" or "Whatever you want." Write what you actually meant. | Translate the performance |
| 8 | Write: "The version of me that no one needs anything from wants \_\_\_." | Introduce the authentic self |

**Monthly Milestone:** You have a clear, written account of what self-erasure has cost you in one specific relationship.

**Month 3 — Baseline Establishment**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 9 | Once per day, state a preference — any preference — without qualifying it. | Build preference expression |
| 10 | Decline one request this week without over-explaining. Note the discomfort. | Practise the boundary |
| 11 | Do one thing this week solely because you want to — not because it helps anyone. | Practise self-directed desire |
| 12 | Write your Q1 reflection: What performance did you see? What surprised you? | Consolidate awareness |

|---|---|---|

| 15 | In the relationship identified in Q1, say one true thing you have been withholding. | Break the performance in the hardest relationship |

| 17 | Tell one person what you actually want from them — not what you think they can give. | Practise direct desire expression |

**Month 5 — Desire Inventory**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 18 | Each day, complete the sentence: "Today I wanted \_\_\_." Do not edit for reasonableness. | Build desire vocabulary |
| 19 | Identify one desire you have had for more than a year that you have not acted on. Write why. | Surface the suppressed want |
| 20 | Take one step toward that desire this week — however small. | Act on authentic desire |
| 21 | Ask someone close to you: "Do you know what I actually want? Not what I say I want — what I want." | External calibration |

**Monthly Milestone:** You have taken one concrete action toward something you want that has nothing to do with what anyone else needs.

**Month 6 — Identity Without Audience**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 22 | Spend one hour per week doing something with no audience — no sharing, no documenting, no reporting. | Build private identity |
| 23 | Write a one-page answer to: "Who am I when no one needs anything from me?" | Define the self |
| 24 | Share that answer with one person. Notice if you edit it for their reaction. | Test the authenticity |
| 25 | Identify one value you hold that is not shared by the people whose approval you seek. Claim it. | Differentiate the self |
| 26 | **Q2 Integration Checkpoint:** What did you stop performing? What are you still performing? | Consolidate disruption |

**Month 9 — Identity Consolidation**

## Archetype 4 — The Seeker
*"You are always searching. This year, you stop searching and start building."*

**Growth Direction:** From insight-collection and commitment-avoidance → toward sustained, embodied action.

---

### Q1 — Awareness (Weeks 1–13): Seeing the Search

**Month 1 — Mapping the Pattern**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | List every framework, personality system, or self-help resource you have engaged with in the past two years. | Inventory the search |
| 2 | For each item on the list, write: "What did I do differently as a result?" | Expose the insight-action gap |
| 3 | Identify the insight you have had most often — the one that never seems to produce change. Write it. | Name the stuck insight |
| 4 | Ask someone who knows you well: "Do you think I act on what I know about myself?" | External calibration |

**Monthly Milestone:** You can articulate the specific insight you have had repeatedly without acting on it.

**Month 2 — Understanding the Cost**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Write a one-page account of a commitment you have been "almost ready" to make for more than a year. | Make the avoidance concrete |
| 6 | Identify the belief underneath the search: "Once I understand myself well enough, then \_\_\_." Write it. | Name the false prerequisite |
| 7 | Notice every time you seek a new framework or explanation instead of taking an action. Count the instances. | Quantify the avoidance |
| 8 | Write: "The version of me that acts without full understanding would \_\_\_." | Introduce the action identity |

**Monthly Milestone:** You have a clear, written account of what insight-without-action has cost you in one specific domain.

**Month 3 — Baseline Establishment**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 9 | For every insight you record this week, write one specific action it implies. Do the action within 24 hours. | Build the insight-action habit |
| 10 | Choose one area of life and make one decision in it. Do not revisit the decision for 30 days. | Practise commitment |
| 11 | Go one week without consuming any new self-development content. Apply what you already know. | Practise sufficiency |
| 12 | Write your Q1 reflection: What search did you see? What surprised you? | Consolidate awareness |
| 13 | **Q1 Integration Checkpoint:** Identify the commitment you have been avoiding longest. This is the Q2 focus. | Transition to action |

### Q2 — Disruption (Weeks 14–26): From Insight to Action

**Month 4 — The Framework Fast**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|

| 17 | At the end of each day, write: "Today I acted on what I know by \_\_\_." | Build action accountability |

**Month 5 — Embodiment**

| Week | Habit | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 18 | Each day, do one thing with your body — not your mind. Physical, sensory, present. | Build embodied presence |
| 19 | Identify one insight that has lived only in your head. Find one physical way to express it. | Embody the insight |
| 20 | Spend one hour per week in an activity that requires presence and cannot be intellectualised. | Practise non-intellectual engagement |
| 21 | Ask someone: "Do I seem present to you, or do I seem like I'm always somewhere else?" | External embodiment audit |

## Implementation Notes

**AI Twin Integration:** The Weekly Check-In (3 questions) runs every week of the year. The AI Twin uses the answers to track progress, identify regression, and adjust its tone and challenge level accordingly. By Week 52, the Twin has 52 data points and can produce a genuinely personalised year-end assessment.

**Tier Gating:** The full 365-day plan is a Deep tier feature. Core tier users receive Q1 only (the awareness quarter). Social tier users receive Q1 and Q2. Deep tier users receive the full year.

**Notification Cadence:** Weekly check-in prompts are sent every Monday. Monthly milestone reminders are sent on the first of each month. Quarter-end checkpoints are scheduled automatically at Weeks 13, 26, 39, and 52.

**Non-Linear Use:** Users who miss weeks do not "fall behind." The plan is designed to be re-entered at any point. The AI Twin tracks the last completed week and resumes from there.
