# MirrorMind — Year 2 Plan Generation: A Detailed Breakdown

**Document type:** Product design specification  
**Applies to:** Deep tier users who complete Week 52 of the 365-Day Archetype Growth Plan  
**Author:** Manus AI

---

## Overview

The Year 2 Plan is not a reset. It is not a recycled version of Year 1 with harder habits. It is the first genuinely personalised output the product produces — because it is the first output built entirely from *what the user actually did*, rather than from what they reported about themselves at intake.

Year 1 begins with a self-report: the Mirror Report intake survey, the friend survey responses, and the initial archetype assignment. These are the best available inputs at the start of the relationship. They are accurate in the aggregate but limited in one important way — they describe the user's pattern as it exists at a single point in time, filtered through self-perception and social desirability.

By Week 52, the Twin has something categorically different: **52 weekly check-in responses, four structured checkpoint reviews, one Year-End Reflection, and external validation from three people in the user's life.** The Year 2 Plan is generated from this accumulated behavioural record, not from a survey. This is what makes it defensible as a product differentiator — no other tool in the market produces a second-year plan with this depth of input.

## Part 1 — The Data Inputs

The Twin draws on six distinct data sources when generating the Year 2 Plan. Each source contributes a different type of signal.

### 1.1 The 52 Weekly Check-In Responses

Every Monday throughout Year 1, the Twin asks three questions. The responses accumulate into a 52-entry behavioural log. The Twin analyses this log for three patterns:

- **Consistency** — which habits were completed reliably across multiple quarters, indicating genuine integration
- **Avoidance clusters** — which habit categories were repeatedly skipped or minimised, indicating persistent resistance
- **Inflection points** — specific weeks where the user's language shifted noticeably, suggesting a meaningful event or realisation

The weekly check-in log is the highest-volume data source and the most behaviourally honest, because users answering a brief Monday check-in are less likely to be performing than users completing a formal intake survey.

### 1.2 The Four Quarterly Checkpoint Reviews

Each of the four Q Integration Checkpoints produces a structured record of four answers: what changed, what was avoided, what surprised the user, and what the forward commitment was. The Twin uses the checkpoint record to construct a **longitudinal pattern map** — a picture of how the user's relationship with their shadow pattern evolved across the four quarters.

The most diagnostic comparison is between the Q1 and Q4 checkpoint answers to the same question. A user who answered "I avoided confrontation in my closest relationships" at Q1 and "I avoided confrontation with my manager but was direct with my partner" at Q4 has made partial, domain-specific progress. The Year 2 Plan targets the remaining domain — the manager — rather than repeating work already done.

### 1.3 The Forward Commitments (Four Stored)

Each checkpoint produced one stored forward commitment. By Week 52, there are four verbatim commitments in the user's profile. The Twin evaluates each one against the subsequent checkpoint's opening response to determine whether it was fulfilled. The fulfilment rate across four commitments is one of the strongest predictors of which Year 2 approach will be most effective:

| Fulfilment Pattern | Year 2 Approach |
|---|---|
| 4 of 4 fulfilled | Expansion — Year 2 targets the hardest relational frontier |
| 3 of 4 fulfilled | Deepening — Year 2 consolidates the strongest gains and addresses the one persistent gap |
| 2 of 4 fulfilled | Stabilisation — Year 2 rebuilds the habit structure with lower-friction entry points |
| 0–1 of 4 fulfilled | Recalibration — Year 2 begins with a full archetype review; the plan may have been misaligned from the start |

### 1.4 The Year-End Reflection Document

The Week 52 Year-End Review produces a structured reflection document with five components: the user's re-read of their Week 1 journal entry, their written response from who they are now, the external validation responses from three people, a self-assessed distance-travelled score, and a statement of the one thing that most changed. The Twin uses this document to calibrate the emotional register of the Year 2 Plan — specifically, whether the user is ready for challenge, needs consolidation, or is experiencing growth fatigue.

### 1.5 External Validation Responses

The three external validation responses — collected from people the user nominated — are the only data source in the entire product that comes from outside the user's own perception. They are also the most difficult to fake. When the external responses align with the user's self-assessment, the Twin treats the Year 1 growth as verified. When they diverge — when the user believes they changed significantly but the external responses describe little visible change — the Twin flags this as the primary focus for Year 2.

This divergence is not treated as failure. It is treated as the most important signal in the dataset: the user has done internal work that has not yet translated into observable behaviour. Year 2 becomes the year of externalisation.

### 1.6 Archetype Stability Record

Across four checkpoints, the Twin tracked whether the original archetype assignment remained stable or whether secondary archetype signals emerged. By Week 52, the Twin has a clear picture of whether the user is a stable single-archetype profile or a dual-archetype profile — and which archetype is dominant under stress versus under growth conditions. Year 2 is built around this nuanced profile, not the original single-archetype assignment.

## Part 2 — The Twin's Synthesis Logic

The Twin processes the six data sources through four analytical steps before generating the Year 2 Plan.

### Step 1 — Growth Domain Mapping

The Twin categorises all Year 1 habits and checkpoint responses into five growth domains: **Self-Awareness**, **Relational Behaviour**, **Professional Conduct**, **Internal Regulation**, and **Identity Expression**. For each domain, it assigns a growth status:

| Status | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Integrated** | Habit is now structural — the user no longer needs a weekly prompt to maintain it |
| **Active** | Habit is practised but not yet automatic — still requires deliberate effort |
| **Resistant** | Habit was repeatedly avoided or minimised — the domain is defended |
| **Untouched** | The domain was not addressed in Year 1 — either by design or by avoidance |

Year 2 does not revisit Integrated domains. It deepens Active domains, directly confronts Resistant domains, and introduces Untouched domains in a sequenced order based on the archetype's growth logic.

### Step 2 — Shadow Pattern Evolution Assessment

The Twin compares the user's shadow pattern description from the Week 1 intake against the behavioural evidence from 52 weeks of check-ins. It produces a **shadow evolution score** on a four-point scale:

- **Unchanged** — the pattern is as entrenched as it was at Week 1
- **Aware but active** — the user can name the pattern in real time but has not yet interrupted it consistently
- **Interrupted** — the user regularly catches and redirects the pattern before it plays out fully
- **Integrated** — the pattern no longer operates automatically; the user has built a genuine alternative response

The shadow evolution score determines the Year 2 starting point. A user at "Interrupted" begins Year 2 in the Integration phase, not the Awareness phase. A user at "Unchanged" begins Year 2 with a fundamentally different approach — the Twin investigates why Year 1 did not move the needle and builds Year 2 around removing that specific obstacle.

### Step 3 — Relational Frontier Identification

Every archetype has a hardest relational frontier — the specific relationship context where the shadow pattern is most entrenched and most defended. For **The Architect**, it is typically the intimate partnership, where the cost of being wrong feels highest. For **The Performer**, it is the professional relationship where the approval stakes are highest. For **The Rebel**, it is any long-term commitment that requires sustained presence rather than periodic intensity.

The Twin identifies the user's specific relational frontier by cross-referencing the avoidance patterns in the checkpoint responses with the external validation responses. The person the user avoided being direct with, the commitment they kept deferring, the relationship they described in the most guarded language — these are the signals. Year 2 is oriented toward this frontier.

### Step 4 — Year 2 Theme Selection

Based on the three preceding steps, the Twin selects one of four Year 2 themes. The theme determines the overall arc of the year — the four-quarter structure remains the same, but the content of each quarter is built around the selected theme.

| Year 2 Theme | Trigger Conditions | Year Arc |
|---|---|---|
| **Externalisation** | Internal growth verified but not yet visible to others | Q1: Making the internal external · Q2: Relational testing · Q3: Social integration · Q4: Identity consolidation |
| **Deepening** | Strong gains in 3 of 5 domains, one persistent resistant domain | Q1: Investigating the resistant domain · Q2: Targeted disruption · Q3: Integration under pressure · Q4: Full-domain synthesis |
| **Expansion** | All domains Active or Integrated, shadow at Interrupted or Integrated | Q1: The hardest relational frontier · Q2: Professional domain challenge · Q3: Identity expression · Q4: Legacy and contribution |
| **Recalibration** | Low fulfilment rate, shadow Unchanged or Aware but active | Q1: Archetype re-examination · Q2: Structural habit redesign · Q3: Friction removal · Q4: Recommitment |

## Part 3 — The Year 2 Plan Output Structure

The Year 2 Plan is delivered as a structured document at the end of the Week 52 Year-End Review session. It contains seven components.

### Component 1 — Year 1 Summary

A two-paragraph summary of the user's Year 1 journey written in the Twin's voice. It names the three most significant changes observed in the data, the one domain that remained resistant, and the forward commitment from Q4 that will carry into Year 2. This is not a generic summary — it references specific check-in responses and checkpoint answers by week number.

### Component 2 — Archetype Profile Update

If the archetype remained stable across all four checkpoints, this section confirms the original assignment and notes any secondary archetype signals that emerged. If the archetype evolved, this section describes the updated profile — which archetype is dominant under stress, which under growth, and how the dual-archetype dynamic will be addressed in Year 2.

### Component 3 — Year 2 Theme and Rationale

A clear statement of the selected Year 2 theme, with a one-paragraph explanation of why the Twin selected it based on the specific data from the user's Year 1 record. The rationale references the shadow evolution score, the growth domain map, and the relational frontier identification.

### Component 4 — The Four-Quarter Year 2 Arc

A quarter-by-quarter overview of the year, with each quarter's theme, focus domain, and the specific relational or behavioural frontier it targets. This is the structural skeleton — the weekly habits are generated progressively rather than all at once, because the Twin needs the Week 13 checkpoint response to calibrate Q2.

### Component 5 — Week 1 of Year 2

The first week's habit is delivered immediately, along with its stated purpose and its connection to the Year 2 theme. The user does not wait — the plan begins the Monday after the Year-End Review.

### Component 6 — The Year 2 Forward Commitment

Before the Year-End Review closes, the Twin asks the user to name one commitment for Year 2 — not a habit, but a relationship or domain they are committing to showing up differently in. This commitment is stored and referenced at the Q1 Checkpoint of Year 2, continuing the accountability loop without interruption.

### Component 7 — The Growth Distance Statement

A single paragraph, written in the second person by the Twin, describing the distance between who the user was at Week 1 of Year 1 and who they are now. This is the most emotionally resonant component of the output and the most likely to be shared — it is written to be read aloud or sent to someone who matters to the user. It is also the most powerful retention mechanism in the product: a user who has received a Growth Distance Statement has a concrete, personal record of what a year of deliberate work produced.

## Part 4 — Archetype-Specific Year 2 Directions

Each archetype's Year 2 has a distinct orientation based on where Year 1 typically leaves them and what the next frontier of growth requires.

| Archetype | Typical Year 1 Outcome | Year 2 Primary Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **The Architect** | Reduced analysis paralysis in low-stakes decisions; high-stakes decisions still over-prepared | Making the commitment they have been structuring around for years — typically a relationship, career, or creative commitment |
| **The Performer** | Reduced performance in safe relationships; professional performance largely intact | Bringing authenticity into the professional context where the approval stakes are highest |
| **The Protector** | Reduced armour in one close relationship; still cannot ask for help in professional contexts | Learning to be visibly uncertain in professional settings — the domain where strength-as-identity is most defended |
| **The Seeker** | Increased action in low-commitment domains; still avoids the one commitment that would most change their life | Making and sustaining the single most significant commitment they have been researching instead of doing |
| **The Diplomat** | More direct in one-on-one conversations; still softens in group or professional settings | Saying the truest thing they have never said in the context where it matters most — typically a team, family, or public setting |
| **The Visionary** | Completed one significant project; still abandons projects when the novelty phase ends | Building the system that makes completion automatic — removing the dependence on inspiration as the fuel for finishing |
| **The Anchor** | Made one significant change; still defaults to stability when the change feels irreversible | Taking the first irreversible step toward the change that would most alter the trajectory of their life |
| **The Rebel** | Reduced reactive opposition in low-stakes contexts; still cannot sustain commitment when it requires invisibility | Staying in the room — the specific commitment, relationship, or project that requires sustained presence without recognition |

## Part 5 — Product and Business Implications

The Year 2 Plan is the product's most powerful retention mechanism because it converts a one-year purchase decision into a continuous relationship. A user who receives a Year 2 Plan has not completed MirrorMind — they have graduated to a more sophisticated version of it.

The compounding value proposition is also the most defensible competitive moat. A competitor can replicate the intake survey, the archetype framework, and the weekly habit structure. They cannot replicate 52 weeks of a specific user's behavioural data. The Year 2 Plan is, by definition, impossible to reproduce without the Year 1 record — which means the longer a user stays, the harder it becomes for them to leave.

From a pricing perspective, the Year 2 Plan justifies the Deep tier's annual renewal without requiring a new sales conversation. The renewal prompt is not "subscribe again" — it is "your Year 2 Plan is ready." The user is not being asked to buy a product; they are being invited to continue a journey that is already underway and already producing results they can name.

The Growth Distance Statement serves a secondary function as organic acquisition. A user who shares their Growth Distance Statement — with a partner, a friend, or publicly — is providing the most credible possible testimonial: a specific, personal account of what a year of deliberate work produced. It is not a review. It is evidence.
